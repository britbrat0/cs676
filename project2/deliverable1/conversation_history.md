# Comments


The quality of the conversation stream in each simulation is quite high. Each conversation progresses in a seemingly natural way that mimics a real human-to-human conversation. Each is realistic and coherent and follows the given context appropriately. Each response makes sense given the preceding one and there are no non sequiturs. Responses are grammatically correct and logical.


Each persona responds in a way that is consistent with their defined traits and demographics. For example, Lisa, who is 68 years old and a beginner tech user, says she ‘prefer[s] larger buttons and readable fonts’ and ‘like[s] to learn new apps slowly and carefully’.


However, there are some phrases used by the personas that do not sound very natural. For example, Lisa uses the term ‘interface’ several times. That might now be something a 68-year old person who is not very tech savvy would say. Additionally, his line that Marco says may not be something a real human would say in this situation: ‘...with strong privacy measures like encryption and minimal permissions, is key.’


There is also some repetitiveness and limitation in responses. Some of the personas seem to be stuck on expressing their specific traits and reiterate it in different ways in many of their responses. For example, in almost all of her responses, in different ways, Lisa mentions that she wants to use technology that is easy to learn and not confusing. Due to this, the diversity and depth of insights generated by the different persona types is not as vast as human feedback would be.
